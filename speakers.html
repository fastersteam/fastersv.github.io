Meet Our Speakers

Yobie Benjamin

Keynote Speaker

Currently CTO of Token.io - providing software to banks that enables them to issue a new payment type designed specifically for use in the digital era. Token's solution is the only compliance ready solution to address the European Central Bank's Payment Services Directive II (PSD2). Senior advisor the CEO and Board of Directors — AstroDigital.com (satellites and space imagery, satellite communications), CambrianInnovation.com (clean water and clean power), Stilla (wearables), and Wonderloop (video), WildlifeWorks.com (forest and wildlife conservation/carbon credits), WearableWorld (IoT accelerator), Moni.com (payments/fintech). Mentor to Indie.bio (synthetic biology incubator). Yobie Benjamin sat on Intel Capital's Enterprise Advisory Board for five years till January 2016. As co-founder of Avegant, Yobie has advanced the development of a next generation wearable device — Avegant Glyph — The Smart Headphone and raised $1.5MM on Kickstarter, $9.4MM on Series A and A1 and $24MM on Series B. Yobie was a volunteer civilian advisor and chairman for NASA AMES Collaboration Labs. NASA Ames Research Center, one of ten NASA field enters, is located in the heart of California's Silicon Valley. For more than 75 years, Ames has led NASA in conducting world-class research and development in aeronautics, exploration technology and science aligned with the center's core capabilities. Previously, Yobie was Global Chief Technology Officer at Citigroup ICG/GTS and Chairman of GTS Research Development Innovation & Learning Centres at Citigroup | Institutional Clients Group / Global Transaction Services, with a focus on projects such as Citi Commerce Server, the backend to Google Wallet (now named Android Pay) and Apple Pay. Other major initiatives include proof-of-life in management of Italian and UK pensions, digitizing access to Security and Fund Services. Yobie is a former Partner and Chief of Global Strategy at Ernst & Young LLP and former Chief Knowledge Officer and Director of Enterprise Security Services at Cambridge Technology Partners' Management Labs. Recent Awards: CNN Top 10 Technology Products of 2014 - Avegant Glyph and 2015 Technology Pioneer Award from the World Economic Forum Additional Highlights: Successful venture investor; innovator in the technology, retail and consumer products space; fintech; restaurateur; consumer product design; next generation KYC/AML and anti-fraud systems; systems architect; writer Personal Interests: Synthetic biology, games and game theory, all things food and wine, progressive politics, technology, music, social justice, getting people to vote Companies: Token.io, Avegant, Skyport.com, Gusto.com, Indie Bio, Wearable World, Wildlife Works. Vote.org, Amnesty International Yobie Benjamin graduated from the University of the Philippines.

Michelle Florendo

Michelle Florendo is an engineer by trade, a Type-A management consultant, and a Fortune 500 brand marketer turned career coach. After a decade working alongside corporate professionals, she saw too many peers get stuck in soul-sucking jobs and golden handcuffs. As someone who understood the frustration of high-achieving professionals, Michelle decided to start her own career consulting firm, What If You Could, LLC. She is known for her analytical approach to career consulting - with a BS in Management Science and Engineering from Stanford University, and an MBA from UC Berkeley's Haas School of Business, Michelle uses analytical expertise, careful questioning, and personal experience to help her clients craft fulfilling careers. Over the years Michelle has led workshops both domestically and internationally, and has shown hundreds of driven professionals how to find jobs that satisfy them. Last year she was honored to serve on the inaugural coaching team for Seth Godin's altMBA, a five-week intensive workshop for change-makers around the world. Michelle also works with Management Leadership for Tomorrow, coaching talented underrepresented minorities into careers in tech. She now maintains a portfolio of work that includes personalized 1:1 coaching, group programs, curriculum development, and speaking engagements. Michelle is a winner of the Global FWN100 award, given annually to the 100 most influential Filipinas in the world. She was a member of Stanford’s Pilipino American Student Union (PASU), UC Berkeley’s Pilipino American Graduate Student Association (PAGaSA), and sits on the Advisory Board for Stanford’s Pilipino Alumni Network (SPAN).
