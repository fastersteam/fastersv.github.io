FASTERCON 2016 Speakers

Yobie Benjamin

Keynote Speaker

Yobie is currently CTO of Token.io, which enables banks to issue a new payment type designed specifically for use in the digital era. Token's solution is the only compliance ready solution to address the European Central Bank's Payment Services Directive II (PSD2). He is also a co-founder of Avegant, where he advanced the development of a next generation wearable device — Avegant Glyph — The Smart Headphone and raised $1.5MM on Kickstarter, $9.4MM on Series A and A1 and $24MM on Series B. The product was recognized as one of CNN's Top 10 Technology Products of 2014.

Previously, Yobie was Global Chief Technology Officer of Citigroup Institutional Clients Group / Global Transaction Services, with a focus on projects such as the Citi Commerce Server, the backend to Google Wallet (now Android Pay) and Apple Pay. He was also Chief of Global Strategy at Ernst & Young LLP and former Chief Knowledge Officer and Director of Enterprise Security Services at Cambridge Technology Partners' Management Labs. Yobie was also a volunteer civilian advisor and chairman for NASA AMES Collaboration Labs.

Yobie currently sits on the Board of Directors for a number of companies, which include AstroDigital (satellites and space imagery, satellite communications), CambrianInnovation (clean water and clean power), Stilla (wearables), and Wonderloop (video), WildlifeWorks.com (forest and wildlife conservation/carbon credits), WearableWorld (IoT accelerator), Moni.com (payments/fintech). He also sat on Intel Capital's Enterprise Advisory Board for five years till January 2016. He was awarded in 2015 with the Technology Pioneer Award from the World Economic Forum. Yobie Benjamin graduated from the University of the Philippines.

Patrici Flores

Erin Pangilinan

Erin is a proud Silicon Valley native, born and raised. She is designer and developer hybrid, data creative, and entrepreneur. As founder & CEO of HARP, a stealth startup, she is working at the intersection of Augmented Reality (AR) Virtual Reality (VR), big data, artificial intelligence, and biotech. Erin is also co-founder of ARVR Academy, an organization dedicated to diversity and inclusion in AR and VR through educational and professional development efforts to make ARVR more accessible to people of all backgrounds with an emphasis on women and underrepresented communities. In 2014, Erin’s team placed 1st in the US at DataHack14 winning $5K and an opportunity to join a corporate accelerator program with office space and seed funding. In her previous life, Erin worked in electoral, issue-based, campaigns and nonprofit organizations. She worked as official campaign staff for the former Deputy U.S. Secretary of Commerce, Ro Khanna, to represent her hometown, Fremont and backyard of (CA-District 17), the heart of Silicon Valley and focused on finance, digital strategy, ethnic media, and Filipino American voter outreach. Cal student activist involvement: PAA, PASS, P4, {m}aganda magazine, hardboiled asian american newsmagazine, APASD, theatre rice! Co-founder of Asian Pacific American Coalition (APAC) spearheading the statewide Count-Me-In campaign to disaggregate Asian American Pacific Islander (AAPI) to collect accurate admissions data to support student initiated outreach programs. She is a Warriors fan, Twitch watcher, anime otaku, spoken word + page poet emcee who embraces all the elements of hip hop.

Michelle Florendo

Michelle Florendo is an engineer by trade, a Type-A management consultant, and a Fortune 500 brand marketer turned career coach. After a decade working alongside corporate professionals, she saw too many peers get stuck in soul-sucking jobs and golden handcuffs. As someone who understood the frustration of high-achieving professionals, Michelle decided to start her own career consulting firm, What If You Could, LLC. She is known for her analytical approach to career consulting - with a BS in Management Science and Engineering from Stanford University, and an MBA from UC Berkeley's Haas School of Business, Michelle uses analytical expertise, careful questioning, and personal experience to help her clients craft fulfilling careers. Over the years Michelle has led workshops both domestically and internationally, and has shown hundreds of driven professionals how to find jobs that satisfy them. Last year she was honored to serve on the inaugural coaching team for Seth Godin's altMBA, a five-week intensive workshop for change-makers around the world. Michelle also works with Management Leadership for Tomorrow, coaching talented underrepresented minorities into careers in tech. She now maintains a portfolio of work that includes personalized 1:1 coaching, group programs, curriculum development, and speaking engagements. Michelle is a winner of the Global FWN100 award, given annually to the 100 most influential Filipinas in the world. She was a member of Stanford’s Pilipino American Student Union (PASU), UC Berkeley’s Pilipino American Graduate Student Association (PAGaSA), and sits on the Advisory Board for Stanford’s Pilipino Alumni Network (SPAN).

Anatalio Ubalde

Anatalio Ubalde is an economic developer, entrepreneur, and patent-holder. He is the CEO of GIS Planning Inc., an economic development internet company; ZoomProspector.com, an online corporate site selection platform; and SizeUp, a small business intelligence service. Executives from Facebook, Google, and Salesforce.com, and others selected SizeUp as the 1st place business app to help businesses and grow the economy (jury of Sheryl Sandberg, Vint Cerf, Tim O’Reilly). His company’s online strategies are implemented by economic development organizations in 42 states, serve the majority of the 100 largest cities, and over 60% of Americans. Mr. Ubalde’s work has been featured in The Wall Street Journal, BusinessWeek, Forbes, Fortune, and The New York Times. He is co-author of the books Economic Development Online and Economic Development Marketing. 5 years in a row, GIS Planning made the Inc. 5000 list of fastest growing private companies in the United States. He was named a Fellow Member of IEDC for achieving exceptional stature in the field of economic development. He previously worked in public sector economic development. He is a 26-time United States Masters Diving National Champion and an All-American. Mr. Ubalde received his bachelors and masters degrees from U.C. Berkeley. As an undergrad he was awarded UC Berkeley’s top male scholar athlete, receiving the award during the Big Game bonfire rally. He was a 4-year varsity letterman for the Cal Swimming & Diving Team. As a senior he taught the first course in in the USA about Filipino American Architecture in Cal’s Department of Architecture.
